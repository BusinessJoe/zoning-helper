CLAUSE I - LANDS REGULATED<br>
1.	The lands regulated by this By-law are shown outlined with a heavy black line on Schedule "A" annexed hereto, and the regulations applicable to such lands shall be in accordance with the Clauses and Schedules of this By-law.  <br>
2.	Where lands on Schedule "A" do not have letters or numbers noted thereon, the regulations applicable to such lands shall be as follows:<br>
2.1	<u><b>Streets and Watercourses</b></u><br>
The regulations on lands adjacent to a street or watercourse shall extend to the centre lines of the streets or watercourses.<br>
2.2	<u><b>Lake Ontario</b></u><br>
The regulations on the lands adjacent to Lake Ontario shall extend to the municipal boundary.<br>
CLAUSE II - GENERAL PROVISIONS<br>
1.	<u>Restrictions on the Use of Land and/or Buildings</u><br>
No building, structure or land shall hereafter be used or occupied, and no building or structure, or part thereof, shall be erected, moved or altered except in conformity with provisions of this By-law.<br>
2.	<u>General Exemption</u><br>
Where existing buildings and structures have been lawfully erected and a By-law, or amendments to it, have been subsequently enacted with the result that some characteristic of the building or structure does not conform, but the existing uses conform to the land uses specifically permitted on the parcel by the applicable provisions set out in Schedule "A" and Schedule "C" of the By-law; those provisions of the By-law which cause the non-conformity shall not apply with respect to:<br>
2.1	The reconstruction or renovation of an existing building, or structure which has been damaged or destroyed by fire, explosion, Act of God or any other cause beyond the owner's control, after March 23, 1987, provided that:<br>
2.1.1	The replacement buildings or structures erected on the parcel shall conform to the By-law to the same, or to a greater extent, as those existing on the date the damage occurred, and shall be reconstructed or renovated for the same land use which existed on the parcel on the date the damage occurred; and<br>
2.1.2	In instances where the number of parking spaces supplied on the parcel on the date the damage occurred was insufficient to meet the minimum requirement for parking spaces for the uses which existed on the date the damage occurred, this <b>parking space deficiency</b> shall not be increased as a result of the reconstruction or renovation, and the total number of parking spaces on the parcel shall not be decreased.<br>
A building or structure renovated or reconstructed pursuant to this Section shall be deemed to have been erected on the day of original construction for the purpose of this By-law.<br>
2.2	A change in the use of an existing building or structure to other land uses specifically permitted by the applicable provisions set out in Schedule "A" and Schedule "C" of the By-law, provided that:<br>
2.2.1	The land use changes in the buildings or structures, in combination with all other uses on the parcel, shall not increase the extent of the non-conformity; and<br>
2.2.2	In instances where the existing number of parking spaces supplied on the parcel is insufficient to meet the minimum requirement for parking spaces for the existing uses, this <b>parking space deficiency</b> shall not be increased as a result of the change to the new use, and the total number of parking spaces on the parcel shall not be decreased.<br>
2.3	The construction of additional buildings or structures on a parcel, provided that:<br>
2.3.1	Any additional buildings or structures shall conform to all the provisions of the By-law; and<br>
2.3.2	Any additional buildings, and structures, and uses, in combination with all other buildings, structures, and uses on the parcel, shall not increase the extent of non-conformity; and<br>
2.3.3	In instances where the existing number of parking spaces supplied on the parcel is insufficient to meet the minimum requirement for parking spaces for the existing uses, the <b>parking space deficiency</b> shall not be increased as a result of the additional buildings, structures, and uses, and the total number of parking spaces on the parcel shall not be decreased.<br>
3.	<u>Expansion of Non-Conforming Buildings and Structures</u><br>
Where existing buildings and structures have been lawfully erected and the By-law or amendments to it have been subsequently enacted with the result that some characteristic of the building or structure does not conform, but the existing land uses conform to the land uses specifically permitted on the parcel by the applicable provisions set out in Schedule "A" and Schedule "C" of the By-law:<br>
3.1	The existing buildings and structures may be structurally altered or renovated provided that:<br>
3.1.1	The altered or renovated buildings or structures shall continue to conform to all provisions of the By-law, to which the existing buildings or structures conformed.<br>
3.1.2	Any structural alterations and renovations, in combination with all other buildings and structures on the parcel, shall not increase the extent of non-conformity; and<br>
3.1.3	In instances where the existing number of parking spaces supplied on a <b>parcel</b> is insufficient to meet the minimum requirement for parking spaces for the existing use, this <b>parking space deficiency</b> shall not be increased as a result of the alterations or renovations and the total number of parking spaces on the parcel shall not be decreased.<br>
3.2	The existing buildings and structures may be enlarged, provided that:<br>
3.2.1	Any enlargement to buildings or structures shall conform to all the provisions of the By-law; and<br>
3.2.2	   The enlargement, in combination with all other buildings, structures, and uses on the parcel, shall not increase the extent of non-conformity; and<br>
3.2.3	In instances where the existing number of parking spaces supplied on the parcel is insufficient to meet the minimum requirement for parking spaces for the existing uses, this <b>parking space deficiency </b>shall not be increased as a result of the enlargement and the total number of parking spaces on the parcel shall not be decreased.<br>
4.	<u>Parking Space Deficiency</u><br>
For the purposes of the regulations in this Clause, a <b>parking space deficiency</b> occurs when the number of parking spaces provided for the uses on a parcel of land is less than the number of parking spaces required for these uses.  The extent of the <b>parking space deficiency</b> shall be calculated by subtracting the number of parking spaces provided from the number of parking spaces required.<br>
5.	<u>Second Suite Permission when the Existing Use is Legal Non-Conforming</u><br>
When the existing use is legal non-conforming, a <b>second suite</b> may be provided in the existing detached <b>single-family dwellings</b> and existing <b>semi-detached/two family dwellings</b>, notwithstanding Section 3.  <u><b>Expansion of Non-Conforming Buildings and Structures</b></u> of <b>CLAUSE II-GENERAL PROVISIONS</b>.  A <b>parking space</b> required for the <b>second suite </b>shall be provided.<br>

CLAUSE V - INTERPRETATION<br>
To interpret the restrictions applicable to any particular parcel of land or building the following procedure shall be adopted:<br>
(a)	Refer to the regulations applicable to all areas restricted by this By-law as found in Clauses II, VI, and VII.<br>
(b)	Refer to Schedule "A" and note the characters (letters and numbers) applicable to the particular area under consideration (subject area bounded by heavy black line).<br>
(c)	The letter or letters refer to one of the zones established by Clause IV of this By-law.  Uses Permitted in each of the Zones are set forth in respective Sub-Clauses of Clause VIII of this By-law under appropriate headings.  For example, "M" refers to the Multiple-Family Residential provisions in Sub-Clause 3; "S" refers to the Single-Family Residential provisions in Sub-Clause 1, etc.<br>
(d)	The number or numbers in Schedule "A" refer to required standards of performance specific details of which shall be ascertained by reference to the Performance Standard Chart (Schedule "B").<br>
(e)	Refer to Schedule "C" for specific exceptions to the provisions of this By-law.<br>
(f)	<u>Definitions</u><br>
<b>Accessory Building</b><br>
shall mean a subordinate building or portion of the main building the use of which is incidental to that of the main or principal building.<br>
<b>Accessory Sign</b><br>
shall mean a sign that relates exclusively to the use of the premises on which the sign is situated.<br>
<b>Adult Entertainment Parlours</b><br>
shall mean any premises or part thereof in which is provided in pursuance of a trade calling, business or occupation, goods or services appealing to or designed to appeal to erotic or sexual appetites or inclinations.<br>
<b>Amenity Space</b><br>
shall mean indoor or outdoor space on a lot that is communal and available for use by the occupants of a building on the lot for recreational or social activities.<br>
<b>Angular Plane</b><br>
shall mean an imaginary flat surface projecting over a lot, at an inclined angle measure up from the horizontal.	<br>
<b>Apartment Building</b><br>
shall mean a building arranged, intended or designed to be occupied by five or more <b>families</b> living independently of each other.<br>
<b>Art Gallery</b><br>
means premises used for the exhibition, collection or preservation of works of art for public viewing. <br>
<b>Artist Studio</b><br>
shall mean premises used for creating art or craft.<br>
<b>Automated Banking Machine</b><br>
shall mean a device at which customers can complete self-serve financial transactions.<br>
<b>Bachelor Suite</b><br>
shall mean a self-contained dwelling unit in an apartment building, consisting of a bed-sitting room combination, a kitchen, a bathroom and spaces normally accessory thereto.<br>
<b>Banquet Hall</b><br>
shall mean a place of assembly for the purpose of consumption of food or beverages and, without limiting the generality of the foregoing, shall include restaurants, beverage rooms, taverns, and cafeterias.<br>
<b>Basement</b><br>
shall mean a storey or portion of a storey of a building in which the floor is more than .75 m below the level of the finished grade of the averaged finished grade at the front and side main walls of the building.<br>
<b>Basement Height</b><br>
shall be measured from the averaged finished grade at the front main wall of the building to the ceiling of the basement.  If there is no ceiling, the measurement shall be made to the underside of the floor joists.<br>
<b>Bicycle Parking Space</b><br>
		shall mean an area used for parking or storing a bicyle.<br>
<b>	Building Setback</b><br>
shall mean a horizontal distance measured at a right angle from any <b>lot line</b> to the nearest part of the main wall of a building or structure. <br>
<b>Circular Driveway</b><br>
means a <b>driveway</b> having two access points to a <b>street</b>.<br>
<b>Coverage</b><br>
shall mean the area of all buildings and structures on a <b>lot</b> or parcel measured to the exterior limit of all roof lines excluding the following projections:<br>
1.	Any eaves and roof overhangs up to a maximum of 1 m.<br>
2.	Any eaves, roof overhangs and canopies over unenclosed pedestrian walkways.<br>
<b>Day Nursery</b><br>
means the provision of temporary care or guidance or both temporary care and guidance, for a continuous period not exceeding twenty-four hours to more than five children who are:<br>
(i)	Not of common parentage, and<br>
(ii)	Under eighteen years of age in the case of children with a developmental handicap, and<br>
(iii)	Under ten years of age in all other cases.<br>
<b>Domestic or Household Arts</b><br>
means any domestic or household art, the practice of which does not affect the amenity of the neighbourhood.<br>
<b>Drive-through Facility</b><br>
means the use of land, buildings or structures, or parts thereof, to provide or dispense products or services, either wholly or in part, through an attendant or a window or an automated machine, to persons remaining in motorized vehicles that are in a designated stacking lane. A drive-through facility may be in combination with other uses such as, a bank, dry cleaning and laundry collecting establishment, financial institution, personal service shop, restaurant, retail store, service station, or take-out restaurant. A <b>Drive-through Facility </b>does not include a Mechanical or Automatic Car Wash or Automobile Service Station.<br>
<b>Driveway</b><br>
means any surface prepared with paving materials and designed for or used by <b>vehicles </b>to travel over.<br>
<b>Dwelling Unit </b>or<b> Dwelling</b><br>
means a separate set of living quarters designed or intended for use or used by an individual or by one <b>family</b> only and shall include:<br>
1.	at least one room; and<br>
2.	a separate kitchen; and<br>
3.	sanitary conveniences; and<br>
shall have a private entrance from outside the building or from a common hallway or staircase.  <br>
In addition, it may include a <b>second suite</b> where there is no addition to nor substantial alteration to the exterior appearance of the front or side of the <b>dwelling unit</b> facing a street as a result of the introduction of the <b>second suite</b>, such as but not limited to, a new private entrance for the <b>second suite</b> in any <b>main walls</b> facing a <b>street yard</b>.<br>
<b>Eating Establishment</b><br>
shall mean premises where food or beverages are prepared and offered for sale to patrons for immediate consumption on the premises while they are seated, and which may include an incidental take-out service. <br>
<b>Family</b><br>
means one or more persons living as a single housekeeping unit in a <b>dwelling</b>, and may include domestic servants and not more than two roomers or boarders, but does not include persons living together in a <b>group home</b>, <b>correctional group home</b> or a <b>residential care facility</b>.<br>
<b>Floor Area</b><br>
shall mean the area of all floors of a dwelling measured from the outside of the exterior walls, excluding the floor area of all basements and garages.<br>
<b>Front Lot Line</b><br>
shall mean the line which divides a lot from a street.  On a corner lot, the shortest of the lines which divide the lot from a street shall be deemed to be the front lot line.<br>
<b>Front Yard</b><br>
shall mean the space between a main front wall of a building and a front lot line, and extending the full width of the lot.<br>
<b>Games Arcade</b><br>
shall mean an establishment where one or more electronic or manually operated games machines are used for recreation, entertainment or amusement. A bowling alley, billiard hall or pool hall is not considered to be a <b>Games Arcade</b>.<br>
<b>Green Roof</b><br>
shall mean an extension of a building's roof that allows vegetation to grow in a growing medium and which is designed, constructed and maintained in compliance with the Toronto Green Roof Construction Standard set out in Municipal Code Chapter 492. <br>
<b>Gross Floor Area</b><br>
shall mean the area of all floors, including <b>basements </b>and mezzanine floors, except that storage rooms and washrooms located in the <b>basement</b>, parking structures including ramps and <b>driveways</b>, and <b>utility rooms </b>shall not be included.  <b>Gross floor area</b> shall be measured to the external faces of the exterior walls, except that <b>gross floor area </b>of a use occupying a portion of a building shall be measured to the centre line of dividing walls and the external faces of the exterior walls.  Where there is no dividing wall between uses, the <b>gross floor area </b>shall be measured to the dividing line between uses.<br>
<b>Ground Floor Area</b><br>
shall mean the area of the building based on exterior dimensions of the said building or buildings.<br>
<b>Ground Sign</b><br>
shall mean a sign directly supported by the ground without aid of any other building or structure, other than the sign structure.<br>
<b>Group Home</b><br>
means premises used to provide supervised living accommodation, licensed or funded under Province of Ontario or Government of Canada legislation, for up to ten persons, exclusive of staff, living together in a single housekeeping unit because they require a supervised group living arrangement.<br>
<b>Group Home, Correctional</b><br>
means a <b>Group Home</b> operated for persons who have been placed on probation, released on parole or admitted for correctional purposes;<br>
<b>Hammerhead Turnaround</b><br>
means that portion of a <b>driveway</b> designed or intended to permit a <b>vehicle </b>to complete a 180<sup>o</sup> turn on the <b>driveway</b>.<br>
<b>Height</b><br>
shall mean the vertical distance measured from the mean grade at the intersection of the side lot lines and the minimum front yard setback to the highest point of the building, excluding chimneys, skylights, vents, antennae, elevator machine rooms, and parapet walls.<br>
<b>Loading Space</b><br>
shallmean an area used for the loading or unloading of goods or commodities from a vehicle.<br>
<b>Lot Depth</b><br>
shall mean the distance between the midpoint of the front lot line and the midpoint of the rear lot line.<br>
<b>Lot Line	</b><br>
shall mean any boundary of a lot<br>
<b>Main Wall</b><br>
shall mean the exterior front, side or rear wall of a building, and all structural members essential to the support of a fully or partly enclosed space or roof.  The following projections, to the maximum distances shown below, shall not be considered part of the main wall, except that within "NC", "CC" and "HC" Zones, no such projections shall extend into a yard which is required to separate a building from a street:<br>
Chimneys, pilasters and <br>
projecting columns						500 mm<br>
Roof overhang							1 m<br>
Balconies and unenclosed porches <br>
projecting into a front yard, rear<br>
yard or a side yard abutting a street		1.55 m<br>
Exterior steps							No Limit<br>
<b>Marketplace Sign</b><br>
shall mean a non-accessory ground-mounted sign such as a pedestal or pillar ad sign and does not include signage on benches, telephone booths, transit shelters or other similar objects.<br>
<b>Massage Therapy</b><br>
shall mean premises providing <b>massage therapy</b> by persons who are medical or health professionals licensed or registered under Province of Ontario legislation. A body rub service or wellness centre is not a <b>massage therapy</b>.<br>
<b>Museum</b><br>
shall mean premises used for the exhibition, collection or preservation of objects of cultural, historical or scientific interest for public viewing.<br>
<b>Multiple-Family Dwellings</b><br>
means low density <b>family</b> type rental <b>dwelling units</b>.<br>
<b>Non-Accessory Sign</b><br>
shall mean a sign which does not relate to the use of the premises on which the sign is situated, or which is not an <b>accessory sign</b> or a transit shelter advertising sign.<br>
<b>Park</b><br>
shall mean land or structures owned and operated by a public authority for the provision of recreational, cultural and conservation activities, facilities and services.<br>
<b>Parking Space</b><br>
shall mean any surface prepared with paving material designed for or used for <b>vehicle</b> parking.<br>
<b>Passenger Terminal</b><br>
		shall mean premises used for the boarding or discharge of people being transported.<br>
<b>Performing Arts Studio</b><br>
shall mean premises used for the rehearsal of performing arts, such as music, dance or theatre. <br>
<b>Personal Service Shop</b><br>
shall mean premises used to provide personal grooming services or for the cleaning or care of apparel.<br>
<b>Pet Services</b><br>
shall mean premises used to provide for the grooming of domestic animals. A <b>veterinary hospital</b> or a kennel are not <b>pet services</b>. <br>
<b>Place of Entertainment</b><br>
shall mean premises used as a concert hall, cinema, or theatre.<br>
<b>Place of Worship</b><br>
shall mean land, building or structure, or part of a building or structure, used by bona fide religious groups or organizations for religious purposes and other related uses, save and except cemeteries.<br>
<b>Post Secondary School</b><br>
shall mean premises used for educational purposes by a degree granting college or university under Province of Ontario legislation.<br>
<b>Private Home Day Care</b><br>
shall mean the provision of temporary care and custody of not more than five children under ten years of age for reward or compensation in a private residential <b>dwelling unit</b> other than the home of a parent or guardian of any such child or children for a continuous period not exceeding 24 hours.<br>
<b>Production Studio</b><br>
shall mean premises used for producing live broadcasts, motion pictures, or audio or video recordings or transmissions. The mass reproduction of film or recordings is not a <b>production studio</b>.<br>
<b>Public Transportation Uses</b><br>
shall mean the use of land, buildings or structures, or portions thereof, for the operation of a transportation system which is provided by, or under the control of, a public authority, but excludes a transit vehicle maintenance yard and/or storage facility.<br>
<b>Rear Lot Line</b><br>
shall mean the lot line which is farthest from, and opposite to, the front lot line.  In the case of a triangular lot, the rear lot line is the apex of the angle formed by the intersection of the side lot lines.<br>
<b>Rear Yard</b><br>
shall mean the space between a main wall of a building, and a rear lot line extending the full width of the lot.  The location of accessory buildings shall not be used to determine the dimensions of a rear yard.<br>
<b>Rear Yard Setback</b><br>
shall mean a horizontal distance on a lot measured at a right angle from the <b>rear lot line</b> to the nearest main wall of a building or structure. <br>
<b>Recreational Use</b><br>
shall mean the use of lands, buildings, or structures primarily for the pursuit of sports, games, or physical exercise, and may include associated spectator seating.<br>
<b>Religious Education Use</b><br>
shall mean the use of premises by a religious organization for religious education, other than:<br>
a <b>post-secondary school</b>; or<br>
a school regulated under the Education Act, R.S.O. 1990, c.E.2, as amended.<br>
<b>Residential Care Facility</b><br>
means a supervised facility for the accommodation of more than 10 persons, exclusive of staff who, by reason of their emotional, mental, social or physical condition, or legal status, require a group living arrangement for their well-being, and where:<br>
-	The members of the group are referred by a hospital, court or government agency, or recognized social services agency or health professional; and<br>
-	Such facility is funded wholly or in part by any government, other than funding provided for capital purposes only, or such a facility is licensed or approved under Provincial statute.<br>
Such facility is not a hospital, nursing home, retirement home or convalescent home.<br>
<b>Retail Store</b><br>
		shall mean premises in which goods or commodities are sold, rented or leased. <br>
<b>Rooming and/or Boarding House</b><br>
means living accommodation, provided for valuable consideration to more than two persons who are not members of the single <b>family</b> which normally occupies the <b>dwelling</b> where said accommodation is provided or given.<br>
<b>School</b><br>
shall mean a School as defined under The Education Act, as amended, and under The Education Act, as amended, shall include educational uses, School Board administrative uses and secondary uses for community, cultural, and recreational purposes. <br>
<b>Second Suite</b><br>
shall mean a separate, self-contained residential accommodation located in a detached <b>single-family dwelling</b>, the whole of which prior to the introduction of the <b>second suite</b> is at least 5 years old, and a <b>semi-detached/two family dwelling</b>, the whole of which prior to the introduction of the <b>second suite</b> is at least 5 years old, which shall be smaller than the remaining portion of the <b>Dwelling Unit or Dwelling</b> excluding garages and accessory buildings, and which shall contain at least one room, a kitchen and separate sanitary conveniences.<br>
<b>Setbacks</b><br>
shall mean the horizontal distance measured at right angles from the lot line to the main wall.<br>
<b>Side Yard</b><br>
shall mean the space between the main side wall of a building, and the side lot line, extending from the front yard to the rear yard.  The location of accessory buildings shall not be used to determine the dimensions of a side yard.<br>
<b>Single-Family Dwelling</b><br>
means a building to be occupied by one <b>family</b> only, having only one kitchen, and including a private garage or carport to accommodate not more than two motor vehicles, may include one <b>second suite</b>, and the usual <b>accessory buildings</b>.<br>
<b>Split Level Dwelling</b><br>
shall mean a dwelling in which the first floor above finished grade is so constructed as to create two or more different levels, the vertical distance between such levels being always less than a full storey.<br>
<b>Stacking Lane</b><br>
means an on-site queuing lane for motorized vehicles which is separated from other vehicular traffic and pedestrian circulation by barriers, markings or signs.<br>
<b>Storey</b><br>
means that portion of a building which is situated between the top of any floor and the top of the floor next above it and, if there is no floor above it, that portion between the top of such floor and the ceiling above it.<br>
<b>Street</b><br>
shall mean a highway as defined by "The Municipal Act".<br>
<b>Street Townhouse</b><br>
shall mean a row of three or more attached <b>dwelling units</b> divided vertically above ground and with each unit having separate frontage on a <b>public street</b>.<br>
<b>Street Yard</b><br>
means the space between a street lot line or lines and the <b>main wall</b> or <b>walls</b> of a building, extending the width and breadth of the lot.<br>
<b>Townhouse </b><br>
means a <b>building </b>that has three or more <b>dwelling units</b>, a<br>
nd no <b>dwelling</b> unit is entirely or partially above another.<br>
<b>Two-Family Dwelling</b><br>
means a building divided vertically into two <b>single-family dwellings</b>.<br>
<b>Utility Room</b><br>
shall mean a room accommodating service facilities for buildings on the same parcel of land and used primarily for one or more of the following purposes:  mechanical, telephone, or electrical services; building maintenance equipment storage; or garbage handling and storage.<br>
<b>Vehicle</b><br>
means a device, either self-propelled or pulled by a self-propelled vehicle, designed to transport persons or goods or for construction, and designed to be supported through contact of wheels or tracks with the ground.<br>
<b>Veterinary Hospital</b><br>
shall mean premises used by a licensed veterinarian for the medical treatment of animals.<br>
<b>Wall Sign</b><br>
shall mean a sign entirely supported by or through the face of a building or structure, or by component members comprising all or a part of said sign face.<br>
<b>Wellness Centre</b><br>
shall mean <b>premises</b> providing services for therapeutic and wellness purposes. A <b>massage therapy</b>, medical office or body rub services is not a <b>wellness centre</b>. <br>
CLAUSE VI - PROVISIONS FOR ALL ZONES<br>
1.	<u>Reduction of Yards</u><br>
No part of a yard or other open space, required about any building for the purpose of complying with the provisions of this By-law, shall be included as a part of a yard or other open space similarly required for another building.<br>
2.	<u>Licences, Permits and Other By-laws</u><br>
Nothing in this By-law shall operate to relieve any person from the obligation to comply with the requirements of the Building By-laws of the Corporation of the City of Toronto, or any other By-laws of the City of Toronto or its predecessors in force from time to time, or the obligation to obtain any licence, permit, authority or approval which may be otherwise lawfully required.<br>
3.	<u>Uses Permitted in All Zones</u><br>
Nothing in this By-law shall prevent the use of any land within the defined area as a public park, public street, or for the erection of public and separate schools within the meaning of the Public and Separate Schools Acts for the Province of Ontario, or for structures essential to the operation of Public Utilities.<br>
4.	<u>Frontage on a Street</u><br>
No person shall erect a building or structure and no person shall use any building, structure, lot or parcel in the defined area, unless the lot or parcel to be so used or upon which the building is situated, erected or proposed to be erected, abuts or fronts on a public street.<br>
5.	<u>Landscaping Requirements</u><br>
In "NC", "CC", "HC", "A" and "M" zones, a 1.5 m strip of land immediately abutting "S" or "T" zones shall not be used for any purpose other than landscaping.<br>
6.	<u>Coverage</u><br>
The maximum <b>coverage</b> for all buildings and structures shall be 33% of the area of the <b>lot</b> or parcel.  Notwithstanding the foregoing, where specific provisions for maximum <b>coverage</b> or maximum <b>gross floor area </b>are provided elsewhere in this By-law, then this Sub-Clause shall not apply.<br>
7.	<u>Day Nurseries in "M" and "A" Zones</u><br>
i)	<b>Day Nurseries</b> shall only be located on or below the second storey.<br>
ii)	<b>Day Nurseries</b> are prohibited within Single-Family, Semi-Detached, Two-Family and Street Townhouse dwellings in "M" and "A" zones.<br>
8.	<u>Games Arcades and Games Machines</u><br>
<u>Incidental to Commercial Operations</u><br>
i)	<b>Games Arcades</b> shall be prohibited except in City Centre Commercial (CCC) and District Commercial (DC) zones, where specified on <b>Schedule "C"</b> or <b>Clause IX</b>.<br>
ii)	Notwithstanding the above, <b>Games Arcades</b> shall be permitted as a use subordinate and incidental to the following principal uses:  bowling alleys, billiard halls, pool halls, and establishments licensed under The Liquor Licence Act except for licensed restaurants within Neighbourhood Commercial (NC) zones.<br>
iii)	Notwithstanding the above, one games machine is permitted per commercial establishment, incidental to the commercial operation, in City Centre Commercial (CCC), District Commercial (DC) and Community Commercial (CC) Zones.<br>
9.	<u>Commercial Recreational/Health Establishments - Prohibitions</u><br>
No one may use or permit the use of any property for a commercial recreational or commercial health establishment either of which invites or is suited to use by prostitutes.<br>
10.	<u>Adult Entertainment Parlours</u><br>
An "adult entertainment parlour" shall be prohibited except in Hotels having fifty or more bedrooms.<br>
11.	<u>Day Nurseries in Schools</u><br>
All private and public educational institutions operated pursuant to The Education Act, R.S.O. 1980, Chapter 129, as amended, and as permitted by this By-law, may use a maximum of 40% of the gross floor area for a Day Nursery.<br>
12.	<u>Non-Accessory Signs</u><br>
12.1	<b>Ground</b> and <b>Wall Signs</b> which are <b>non-accessory</b> are permitted in District Commercial (DC), Community Commercial (CC), Highway Commercial (HC), Neighbourhood Commercial (NC), Office Uses (OU) and Industrial Commercial (MC) zones.<br>
12.2	<b>Ground</b>  and <b>Wall Signs</b> which are <b>non-accessory</b> are prohibited within 400 m of the street line of Highway 401.<br>
12.3	Roof-mounted Signs are prohibited.<br>
12.4	Notwithstanding Sub-Section 12.2, <b>Marketplace Signs</b> are permitted in District Commercial (DC), Community Commercial (CC), Highway Commercial (HC), Neighbourhood Commercial (NC), Office Uses (OU) and Industrial Commercial (MC) zones.<br>
12.4.1	A <b>Marketplace Sign</b> face shall not exceed 2 m in area to a maximum total face area of 8 m for a sign containing four or more faces.<br>
13.	<u>Underground Structures in "NC", "CC", "HC" and "OU" Zones</u><br>
Underground structures in "NC", "CC", "HC" and "OU" Zones shall be set back from streets in accordance with the main wall building setbacks applying within such Zones.<br>
14.	<u>Lands Not Covered by Buildings</u><br>
In areas designated in Schedule "A" as "NC", "CC" and "HC", the land area not covered by buildings shall be used for off-street <b>vehicle </b>parking and landscaping purposes only.<br>
15.	<u>Use of Basements</u><br>
In areas designated on Schedule "A" as "NC", "CC", and "HC", the <b>basement</b> shall be used only for storage purposes for the occupants of the building.<br>

The following <b>front yard</b> landscaping provisions shall apply to <b>street townhouse dwellings</b> where a <b>driveway</b> leads directly to the <b>dwelling unit</b>, <b>single-family dwellings</b>, semi-detached dwellings, <b>two-family dwellings</b>, and duplexes:<br>
For lots with a frontage less than 6 metres, the <b>front yard</b> not covered by a permitted <b>driveway</b> shall be maintained as landscaping.<br>
For lots with a frontage of 6 metres to less than 15 metres, a minimum of 50% of the <b>front yard</b> shall be maintained as landscaping.<br>
For lots with a frontage of 15 metres and greater, a minimum of 60% of the <b>front yard</b> shall be maintained as landscaping.<br>
For the purpose of this subsection, landscaping means trees, shrubs, grass, flowers, vegetables, and other vegetation, decorative stonework, walkways, patios, screening, or other horticultural or landscape-architectural elements, or any combination of these. Landscaping does not include <b>driveways</b> or parking areas, and directly associated elements such as curbs or retaining walls.<br>

The following <b>front yard</b> soft landscaping provisions shall apply to <b>street townhouse dwellings </b>where a <b>driveway</b> leads directly to the <b>dwelling unit, single-family dwellings</b>, semi-detached dwellings, <b>two-family dwellings</b>, and duplexes:<br>
A minimum of 75% of the <b>front yard</b> not covered by a permitted <b>driveway</b> shall be maintained as soft landscaping.<br>
For the purpose of this subsection, soft landscaping means trees, shrubs, grass, flowers, vegetables, and other vegetation, but does not include hard surfaced areas such as but not limited to <b>driveways</b>, parking areas, decorative stonework, walkways, patios, screening or other landscape-architectural elements.<br>

1.2	<u>Application of Parking Rate</u><br>
1.2.1	<u>Calculation of Requirement</u><br>
If a parking rate in the By-law is expressed as a ratio of <b>parking spaces</b> to the <b>gross floor area</b>, the <b>parking space </b>requirement for a use shall be calculated by multiplying the <b>gross floor area</b> of the use by the applicable rate.<br>
1.2.2	<u>Sum of Requirements</u><br>
The total number of <b>parking spaces</b> required on a parcel shall be the sum of the number of <b>parking spaces</b> required for each use on the parcel.  When this sum includes a fraction of a <b>parking space</b>, one additional whole <b>parking space</b> shall be provided.<br>

Except as otherwise specifically regulated by the By-law, parking shall be required for enclosed public walkways and malls in commercial buildings.  For any floor which contains more than one type of use, parking shall be provided for enclosed public walkways and malls at the same rate as is required for retail and service uses.  For any floor which has only one type of use, parking shall be provided for enclosed public walkways and malls at the rate required for that type of use.<br>
1.2.4	<u>Office Lobbies and Walkways</u><br>
For a building which contains both office uses and other commercial uses, if the <b>gross floor area</b> of commercial uses other than offices, exceeds the <b>gross floor area</b> of office uses, parking shall be required for main floor public lobbies and main floor public walkways in accordance with the regulations for enclosed public walkways and malls contained in Section 1.2.3.<br>
1.2.5	<u>Vacant Space</u><br>
When determining the parking supply requirement for vacant space in a building, parking shall be provided in accordance with the requirements corresponding to its intended use.  The most recent of the following shall constitute the intended use of the vacant space:<br>
-	the most recently identified land use in conjunction with any permit issued in accordance with the Building Code Act; or<br>
-	the previous use of the vacant space.<br>
If the vacant space has never been used, and its intended use has never been identified in conjunction with any permit issued in accordance with the Building<br>
Code Act, its intended use shall be deemed to be the permitted use having the lowest minimum parking requirement.<br>

2.1.3	<u>Vehicles Prohibited in Street Yards</u><br>
Parking of <b>vehicles</b> equipped with five or six wheels, excluding spare wheels, designed to support the weight of the <b>vehicle</b> through contact with the ground, is prohibited in <b>street yards</b>.  Notwithstanding this Sub-Section, parking of recreational <b>vehicles</b>, and <b>vehicles</b> for the transport of disabled persons, is permitted in <b>street yards</b> on the <b>driveway</b> leading to the <b>parking space</b> required by this By-law.<br>
2.1.4	<u>Garages, Carports and Accessory Buildings:</u><br>
(all hereinafter termed "Structures")<br>
(a)	Shall not be used for human habitation;<br>
(b)	Shall not exceed a height of 3.7 m measured from the floor of the said structures to the eaves.  This height restriction shall not apply to basement garages;<br>
(c)	May be erected in a side yard, and provided that the structure is distant not less than 1.2 m from any adjoining dwelling, may be located a minimum distance of 300 mm from the side lot line;<br>
(d)	May be erected in rear yard provided that:<br>
(i)		If the structure is partly within the side yard it shall be subject to the provisions of Clause 2.1.4(c);<br>
(ii) 	If the structure so erected is wholly within the rear yard it shall be distant not less than 1.8 m from a dwelling, and shall be distant not less than 450 mm from the nearest lot line.<br>
(iii)	Notwithstanding anything in this By-law, common semi-detached garages may be centred on the side lot line.<br>
(e)	In "S" and "T" zones:<br>
(i)		The maximum dimensions of a garage, carport or free-standing <b>accessory building</b> shall be 7.6 m by 7.6 m.<br>
(ii)	The combined gross floor areas of all garages, carports and free-standing <b>accessory buildings</b> on a lot or parcel shall not exceed 75 m<sup>2</sup>.<br>
(iii)	Notwithstanding Clause 2.1.4(b), the maximum height of all garages, carports and <b>accessory buildings</b> not attached to the <b>dwelling unit</b> shall be 5 m measured from the average finished grade at the perimeter of the building to the highest point of the roof.<br>
(iv)	The gross floor area of all garages, carports or <b>accessory buildings</b> shall not exceed the <b>ground floor area</b> of all such structures.<br>
(v)	In no case shall the <b>ground floor area</b> of any garage, carport or other <b>accessory building</b> be larger than the <b>ground floor area</b> of the principal building on the lot or parcel.<br>
2.2	Regulations for <b>Single-Family Dwellings</b>, Semi-Detached and<br>
<u><b>Two-Family Dwellings</b></u><u>, </u><u><b>Duplexes and Street Townhouse Dwellings</b></u><br>
2.2.1	<u><b>Street Yard</b></u><u> Exceptions</u><br>
2.2.1.1	<b>Vehicles</b> may only be parked in a <b>street yard</b> on that portion of the <b>driveway</b> leading to the <b>parking space</b> required by this By-law.<br>
2.2.1.2	Notwithstanding paragraphs 2.1.1 and 2.2.1.1, the <b>parking space</b> required by this By-law may be located in a <b>street yard</b>, provided that:<br>
1.	Both <b>side yards</b> are less than 2.5 m in width; and<br>
2.	The <b>dwelling unit</b> was constructed prior to September 23, 1985; and<br>
3.	A <b>parking space</b> complying with all other provisions of this By-law was not established on the lot or parcel as of September 23, 1985.<br>
			2.2.1.3	Notwithstanding paragraphs 2.1.1 and 2.2.1.1, the <b>parking space </b>required for a <b>second suite</b> may be located in a <b>street yard</b>, only in tandem on the <b>driveway</b> leading to the <b>parking space</b> required for a dwelling unit.<br>
2.2.2	<u><b>Hammerhead</b></u><u> and </u><u><b>Circular Driveway</b></u><u> Regulations</u><br>
2.2.2.1	<u><b>Hammerhead Turnarounds</b></u><br>
A <b>driveway </b>with a <b>hammerhead turnaround</b> shall be permitted on lots or parcels having frontage greater than 18 m, or on lots or parcels having access to 27 m wide <b>streets</b>, provided that:<br>
where it is located in the <b>front yard</b>, the <b>driveway</b> dimensions, <b>front yard</b> landscaping and <b>front yard</b> soft landscaping requirements of this By-law are complied with; and<br>
where it is located in a <b>street yard</b> that is not a <b>front yard</b>, the <b>driveway</b> and <b>parking space</b> shall not cover more than 60% of the <b>street yard</b>; and <br>
Parking of <b>vehicles</b> is permitted only on that portion of the <b>driveway</b> leading directly to the <b>parking space</b> required by this By-law.<br>
2.2.2.2	<u><b>Circular Driveways</b></u><br>
A <b>circular driveway</b> shall be permitted on lots or parcels having frontage greater than 18 metres, provided that: <br>
(a)	where it is located in the <b>front yard</b>, the <b>driveway</b> dimensions, <b>front yard</b> landscaping and <b>front yard</b> soft landscaping requirements of this By-law are complied with; and<br>
(b)	where it is located in a <b>street yard</b> that is not a <b>front yard</b>, the <b>driveway</b> and <b>parking space</b> shall not cover more than 60% of the <b>street yard</b>.<br>
Parking of <b>vehicles</b> is permitted only on that portion of the <b>driveway</b> leading directly to the <b>parking space</b> required by this By-law.<br>
2.2.3	<u><b>Vehicle</b></u><u> Repair</u><br>
Any work on a <b>vehicle</b> is restricted to maintenance of a <b>vehicle</b> owned by an occupant of the dwelling, and shall exclude painting, sanding, welding and autobody repairs.<br>
